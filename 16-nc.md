# Newton-Cartan

### Confirmed & Polished Summary of Type-I Newton–Cartan (NC)

**Common to both NC and GR**  
- A spacetime is described by a metric structure (a single non-degenerate metric in GR; a pair of degenerate metrics in NC) together with a connection.  
- In GR the metric uniquely determines the (Levi-Civita) connection.  
- In NC the degenerate metrics + compatibility conditions leave residual freedom: the only free connection components are  
  \[
  \Gamma^i_{00}\qquad\text{and}\qquad\Gamma^i_{0j}=-\Gamma^j_{0i}.
  \]
  To fully specify the geometry one must therefore give both the (flat) metrics *and* the free part of the connection.

**Visualization of NC spacetime**  
Absolute time slices are perfectly flat Euclidean 3-spaces (flat sheets of paper stacked along absolute time).  
The metrics \(\tau_\mu\) and \(h^{\mu\nu}\) tell you how to measure time between sheets and distances/angles on each sheet.  
They are not enough to decide how free particles move from one sheet to the next — that is the job of the independent connection.  
When gravity is present the connection bends the free-fall trajectories, so the spacetime is curved (non-zero Riemann tensor of the connection) even though every individual spatial sheet remains flat and time is absolute.

**The free connection components**  
- \(\Gamma^i_{00}\) encodes Newtonian gravity. It is determined by the field equation  
  \[
  R_{\mu\nu}=4\pi G\rho\,\tau_\mu\tau_\nu.
  \]
- \(\Gamma^i_{0j}\) encodes a possible rotational/Coriolis field.  
  Classical Newtonian physics assumes an absolute non-rotating frame exists, so we impose the Trautman condition  
  \[
  R^{[ab]}{}_{cd}=0
  \]
  (coordinate-invariant). This guarantees the existence of coordinates in which \(\Gamma^i_{0j}=0\) everywhere.

**Rotating Earth example**  
In Earth-fixed coordinates both NC and GR have non-zero \(\Gamma^i_{0j}\).  
Most of this is a pure coordinate effect of using a rotating frame.  
- In NC the Trautman condition guarantees we can always transform to a global non-rotating frame where \(\Gamma^i_{0j}=0\).  
- In GR the Earth (or any rotating mass) also produces a small *intrinsic* frame-dragging (Lense-Thirring / gravitomagnetic) field. Even after going to the best non-rotating coordinates, a residual \(\Gamma^i_{0j}\) (or \(g_{0i}\)) remains that cannot be gauged away.  
We deliberately exclude this effect in NC by imposing Trautman, so that the theory stays 100 % equivalent to classical Newtonian gravity.

**Axioms of classical Type-I NC**  
- **A1** Absolute structures: global absolute time + Euclidean geometry on each time slice  
  \(\;\Rightarrow\;\) pair of degenerate *flat* metrics \((\tau_\mu,h^{\mu\nu})\).  
- **A2** Existence of a global non-rotating frame  
  \(\;\Rightarrow\;\) Trautman condition \(R^{[ab]}{}_{cd}=0\).  
- **A3** Field equation  
  \[
  R_{\mu\nu}=4\pi G\rho\,\tau_\mu\tau_\nu.
  \]

In the non-rotating frame given by A2 the only free connection components left are \(\Gamma^i_{00}\), which are completely determined by A3.

**Crucial point (difference from GR)**  
The field equation A3 determines **only the connection**.  
It does *not* determine or curve the metrics.  
The metrics coming from A1 remain absolutely flat for all time; they are fixed background structures.  
Gravity curves only the independent connection that is compatible with those flat metrics.

---

This is precisely how classical Newtonian gravity is geometrised in a fully covariant, coordinate-independent way while remaining 100 % equivalent to the original theory.

# Newton-Cartan using a regulator in metric

It is the formal \(c\to\infty\) limit that recovers Type-I Newton–Cartan geometry from a one-parameter family of ordinary (non-degenerate) Lorentzian metrics. Your description is essentially correct; I will now make it precise and geometric.

### 1. The computational trick formalised

Introduce a one-parameter family of non-degenerate Lorentzian metrics \(g_{\mu\nu}(c)\) that depend on a formal parameter \(c\) (with dimensions of velocity). The standard choice that recovers classical Type-I NC is

\[
g_{\mu\nu}(c)
=
-c^{2}\,\tau_{\mu}\tau_{\nu}
+
h_{\mu\nu}
+
\frac{2\Phi}{c^{0}}\tau_{\mu}\tau_{\nu}
+
O(c^{-2}),
\]

or, more simply in adapted coordinates (\(\tau=\mathrm{d}t\), \(h_{ij}=\delta_{ij}\)):

\[
ds^{2}
=
-\bigl(c^{2}+2\Phi(\mathbf{x})\bigr)\,dt^{2}
+
dx^{2}+dy^{2}+dz^{2}
+
O(c^{-2}).
\]

(The precise higher-order terms can be chosen for convenience; they do not affect the leading Newton–Cartan structures.)

**Procedure**
1. Compute the Levi-Civita connection \(\Gamma^{\lambda}_{\mu\nu}(c)\) of the non-degenerate metric \(g_{\mu\nu}(c)\).  
   All connection coefficients are now uniquely determined (as in GR).
2. Expand every Christoffel symbol in powers of \(1/c\).
3. Take the formal limit \(c\to\infty\).  
   All divergent pieces cancel or are pure gauge, and the finite remainder is exactly the Newton–Cartan connection of Type-I theory:
   \[
   \lim_{c\to\infty}\Gamma^{i}_{00}(c)
   =
   \partial^{i}\Phi,
   \qquad
   \lim_{c\to\infty}\Gamma^{i}_{0j}(c)
   =
   0
   \quad\text{(in the non-rotating frame)},
   \]
   with all other components vanishing or reducing to the flat spatial connection.
4. The degenerate metrics of NC are recovered as the leading pieces:
   \[
   \tau_{\mu}\tau_{\nu}
   =
   -\lim_{c\to\infty}\frac{1}{c^{2}}g_{\mu\nu},
   \qquad
   h^{\mu\nu}
   =
   \lim_{c\to\infty}g^{\mu\nu}.
   \]

The parameter \(c\) is **purely computational**. It is not the physical speed of light; it is a regulator that lets us use a single non-degenerate metric and its unique Levi-Civita connection, then send it to infinity to obtain the degenerate NC structures.

### 2. Geometric picture for large but finite \(c\)

For any finite \(c\) the geometry is an ordinary Lorentzian spacetime (a slight deformation of Minkowski space when \(\Phi\) is weak).  

How do the “flat sheets” connect?

- At infinite \(c\) the light cones are completely flattened into the absolute-time slices: the causal structure becomes the absolute simultaneity of Newton, and the sheets are completely disconnected in the sense that no finite-speed signal can leave its sheet.
- For large but finite \(c\) the light cones are very tall and narrow (opening angle \(\sim 1/c\)).  
  Neighbouring absolute-time sheets are therefore still “almost disconnected,” but they are weakly glued together by the light-cone structure.
- The Newtonian potential \(\Phi\) appears as a small deformation of the \(g_{tt}\) component. This deformation slightly “tilts” or “shears” the way the light cones sit relative to the absolute-time foliation.  
  When one computes the Levi-Civita connection and then takes \(c\to\infty\), that tiny tilt is precisely what survives as the non-zero \(\Gamma^{i}_{00}=\partial^{i}\Phi\).

In other words:

> For finite \(c\) the sheets are connected by ordinary Lorentzian causal structure (light cones).  
> The gravitational field is a gentle warping of those light cones.  
> Sending \(c\to\infty\) freezes the light cones into absolute time slices while the warping of the connection survives as the Newtonian gravitational field.

### 3. Practical rules when using this device

- Whenever you compute a geometric object (connection, curvature, geodesics, …) you must expand in \(1/c\) and keep only the finite piece that survives \(c\to\infty\).
- Distances, angles, and proper times measured with \(g_{\mu\nu}(c)\) only become physically meaningful after the limit is taken (and only the spatial distances defined by \(h_{\mu\nu}\) and the absolute time defined by \(\tau\) survive).
- The final physical theory is still pure Type-I NC: absolute time, flat Euclidean space, curved connection, no post-Newtonian effects, Trautman condition automatically satisfied in the limit if it was imposed on the family, etc.

### 4. Summary of the device

| Step | What you do | Result |
|------|-------------|--------|
| 1 | Write a one-parameter family \(g_{\mu\nu}(c)\) whose leading term is \(-c^{2}\tau\otimes\tau+h\) | Ordinary non-degenerate metric |
| 2 | Compute its unique Levi-Civita connection \(\Gamma(c)\) | Fully determined connection |
| 3 | Expand in \(1/c\) and take \(c\to\infty\) | Exactly the NC connection (\(\Gamma^{i}_{00}=\partial^{i}\Phi\), \(\Gamma^{i}_{0j}=0\) in non-rotating frame) |
| 4 | Extract the leading pieces of \(g_{\mu\nu}(c)\) and \(g^{\mu\nu}(c)\) | The two degenerate flat metrics of NC |

This is a pure computational convenience. It lets you pretend, for a while, that you have a single metric that determines everything (as in GR), encode the Newtonian potential inside that metric via the \(c\)-dependent terms, compute the connection by the usual Christoffel formula, and finally send \(c\to\infty\) to recover the correct Type-I Newton–Cartan geometry.

Geometrically, for large finite \(c\) you have almost-flat absolute-time sheets that are still weakly connected by very tall light cones; the gravitational field is a tiny warping of those cones that becomes the pure \(\Gamma^{i}_{00}\) connection after the limit.
